## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

| Persona | Daily Job | Pain‑Points | Budget (USD) | Typical Decision‑Maker |
|---------|-----------|-------------|--------------|------------------------|
| **Maya, Open‑Source Maintainer** | Maintains 3–5 public repos, triages issues, coordinates community contributions | Scope creep, lack of visibility into backlog, burnout from juggling tasks | $0–$200/month (freemium‑first, optional paid tiers) | Maya herself |
| **Paul, Project Manager (Tech Co‑op)** | Oversees a small internal team that contributes to open‑source libraries | Need to align open‑source work with internal sprint goals, track external contributions | $200–$1,000/month | Paul & CTO |
| **Dan, Developer Advocate** | Builds tools for open‑source community, mentors contributors | Managing feature requests, ensuring quality of contributions, avoiding scope creep | $100–$500/month | Dan & Community Lead |

## Channels & CAC Estimates

| Channel | Primary Activity | CAC (USD) | Rationale |
|---------|------------------|-----------|-----------|
| **GitHub Discussions & Issues** | In‑repo announcements, quick polls, AMA sessions | **$8** | Direct reach to maintainers; low friction |
| **Hacker News & Reddit r/opensource** | Sponsored posts, AMAs, community threads | **$12** | High‑intent audience, strong community engagement |
| **Twitter & LinkedIn** | Thought‑leadership threads, short video demos, paid ads | **$15** | Broad visibility, professional network reach |

*Assumptions:* 1‑2% conversion from each channel, 30‑day funnel, 10% of users convert to paid.
