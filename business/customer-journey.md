## Customer Journey – **open‑source‑guard**  
*Project‑management & scope‑guard for open‑source maintainers*  

| Phase | Trigger Event | Friction Points | User Emotions | Opportunities to Delight | Success Metric |
|-------|---------------|----------------|---------------|--------------------------|----------------|
| **Aware** | • A maintainer reads a blog post about “burnout in open‑source”  <br>• A contributor sees a tweet about “scope‑creep killing projects”  <br>• A CI alert shows a spike in open issues/PRs | • Information overload – many tools claim to “solve” burnout  <br>• Lack of clear “open‑source‑specific” solution | Curiosity → Overwhelm | • Publish a concise, data‑driven one‑pager (e.g., “1 in 5 maintainers quit after 6 months”) <br>• Offer a short explainer video that frames the problem in maintainer‑centric language <br>• Provide a free “burnout self‑check” questionnaire that instantly scores the repo | **Impressions → Click‑through rate** on the self‑check (target ≥ 12 %). |
| **Consider** | • The maintainer receives the self‑check score (e.g., “High risk”) and a recommendation to try open‑source‑guard <br>• A community post lists “Top 5 tools for sustainable OSS” and includes open‑source‑guard | • Uncertainty about integration effort (GitHub, GitLab, self‑hosted) <br>• Concern about open‑source licensing & data privacy | Skepticism → Hopeful | • Provide a **one‑page integration matrix** (GitHub, GitLab, Bitbucket, self‑hosted) with estimated setup time (< 10 min) <br>• Offer a live demo sandbox (no sign‑up) where users can import a sample repo and see scope‑metrics <br>• Publish case‑study snippets with concrete ROI (e.g., “30 % fewer overdue issues in 4 weeks”) | **Demo sign‑ups / sandbox sessions** per visitor (target ≥ 25 %). |
| **Try** | • User clicks “Add to GitHub” or downloads the Docker image and runs the CLI on a test branch | • Installation hiccups (Docker version, missing permissions) <br>• Learning curve of the UI/dashboard <br>• Fear of false‑positive “scope‑creep” alerts | Frustration → Relief (if it works) | • Auto‑generated **step‑by‑step wizard** that validates environment, runs a health‑check, and shows a “first‑run success” banner <br>• In‑app “guided tour” that explains the three core views (Scope Radar, Burnout Index, Prioritisation Board) <br>• Immediate “quick win” suggestion (e.g., “Close stale issue #123”) to prove value | **Trial activation rate** (install → first successful scan) and **time‑to‑first‑insight** (< 2 min). Target ≥ 70 % activation, ≤ 2 min. |
| **Adopt** | • Maintainer sees actionable insights: overdue high‑impact issues, duplicated effort, and a “burnout heat map” for contributors | • Ongoing maintenance of the guard (updates, config drift) <br>• Need to convince core team to keep the tool active | Confidence → Commitment | • Offer **auto‑update hooks** (GitHub Action) that keep the guard current without manual steps <br>• Provide a **team‑role matrix** so each contributor sees only relevant alerts, reducing noise <br>• Release a **monthly health report** (PDF) that can be shared with sponsors or governance boards | **Retention (30‑day active users)** and **frequency of dashboard visits** (≥ 3 visits/week). |
| **Expand** | • Project grows (more contributors, more repos) and the maintainer wants broader coverage | • Scaling to multiple repos, cross‑project dependencies <br>• Need for deeper analytics (e.g., contributor churn prediction) | Pride → Ambition | • Introduce **multi‑repo dashboard** with aggregated scope health <br>• Offer **premium open‑source‑guard extensions** (e.g., AI‑driven task‑prioritisation, integration with funding platforms) <br>• Create a **community marketplace** where maintainers can share custom rule‑sets | **Upsell conversion** to premium extensions (target ≥ 15 % of active adopters) and **number of linked repos** per organization (average ≥ 3). |

### Narrative Flow (quick read)

1. **Aware** – A maintainer discovers the burnout problem via a data‑rich blog post and takes a free self‑check.  
2. **Consider** – The self‑check flags high risk and points to open‑source‑guard; the maintainer reviews a concise integration guide and tries a sandbox demo.  
3. **Try** – With a one‑click install, the guard runs its first scan, instantly surfaces a stale issue, and the user experiences a quick win.  
4. **Adopt** – Regular health reports and low‑noise alerts embed the tool into daily maintainer workflow; the team trusts the data and keeps the guard active.  
5. **Expand** – As the ecosystem scales, the maintainer adds more repos, adopts premium AI‑prioritisation, and shares custom rules with the community, turning open‑source‑guard into a strategic sustainability platform.
